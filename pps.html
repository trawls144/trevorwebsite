Partnership for Public Service

Position: Administrative Fellow
Duration: May 2007 - August 2007
Industry: Non-profit Organization Management
Company Size: 51 - 200 employees

At the Partnership for Public Service, I served as an administrative fellow supporting the organization's mission to revitalize the federal government. This summer fellowship was an early professional experience during my time as a student at Central Michigan University, where I was pursuing a degree in Finance.

The Partnership for Public Service is a nonprofit, nonpartisan organization that works to revitalize the federal government by inspiring a new generation to serve and by transforming the way government works. The organization conducts research, designs solutions, and advocates for reforms to help the government better serve the American people.

As an Administrative Fellow, I provided support to various departments within the organization, gaining exposure to nonprofit operations, government relations, and public policy initiatives. This experience helped me develop foundational professional skills and an understanding of the public sector that would later inform my career path.

Key Achievements

  • Assisted in coordinating events and meetings with government officials and stakeholders, contributing to the organization's outreach efforts
  • Conducted research on federal workforce issues to support policy recommendations and advocacy initiatives
  • Compiled and analyzed data for reports on government performance and best practices
  • Supported administrative functions across multiple departments, gaining broad exposure to nonprofit operations
  • Contributed to the development of communication materials to promote the organization's mission and initiatives
  • Participated in team meetings and strategy sessions, providing input on projects and initiatives
  • Assisted with donor database management and fundraising support activities
  • Developed organizational and analytical skills that would later prove valuable in my marketing and data-focused career
